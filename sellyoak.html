Faith at Selly Oak Hospital

We've left this diary page here in case it might help anyone who stumbles across it. It may be useful if you know someone in hospital with burns, or any serious injury. [2008]

December 2003

Faith living in Oxford.
Thursday 30 October

It's been a while, but a little update. Faith still with her parents, and will be moving into our new flat in a couple of weeks I hope. I'm living there now:

But, for now, and at long long last, you can get hold of Faith direct on MY old mobile number.

Thanks again everyone for keeping in touch, I hope now that people can get in direct contact with her. She's still not using the inter-web thingy either yet.
Wednesday 8 October
Faith leaves hospital!
Friday 3 - Tuesday 7 October

F had a great weekend at home. All went very well, so she should be on course to leave the hospital on Wednesday!

Wednesday 1 - Thursday 2 October (14 weeks)

Well, it looks like I'm nearing the end of my role as journal keeper. If F wants to keep this going that's great, but the surprise news is that she'll probably be leaving the hospital next week! She's going back to her parents for an overnight stay this weekend, and if all goes well they'll throw her out on Wednesday or Thursday (that's the hospital not her parents). Please don't call this weekend though, let's leave it up to Faith initially to contact people, as there's quite a lot to deal with coming home. This is all quite a bit sooner than expected, but F can hobble up and down a flight of stairs, her wounds have healed and there are no respiratory problems, so they want her coping with day to day life again, away from the protection of hospital.

F will probably stay at her parents for a couple of weeks or more, and when she's unblocked her mobile phone (blame me - I was sure I knew what the pin was, but sadly I was a year out) will be in proper contact. F should also be on email again, as her old computer is already at Sutton Courtenay waiting to be installed. So I don't think anyone will actually get to visit Faith in hospital after all this waiting! Some have asked about calling the Burns Unit, but they don't really have facilities for calls for patients, so I think it will have to wait until next week when she'll be back in the world. By November we should be installed in our own new flat, not confirmed, but most likely still in Oxford, and it's more than likely that when she's up to travel, she'll travel around and see as many of you as she can.

Other big news is that F has her mask now. Unsurprisingly she's not a bit happy about it, but she won't have to wear it 24 hrs a day, so if she's goes out, she might not have it on. It might be part of her life for a full year however.
Monday 29 - Tuesday 30 September

F's first bath (hmm, this is getting a bit too much like a baby journal now), and almost all dressings are off now, they remain on her left arm and right leg. Left knee still immobile which is slowing down any progress with walking, but food is staying down!
Sunday 28 September

F had a successful trip out in the Binckes' car with wheelchair. Not the first time outside the hospital, but first time out of Selly Oak anyway. F still not keeping food down yet.
Got a new Ward car now, so back on my own wheels again next week.
Wednesday 24 - Saturday 27 September

Nasal feed now out, but F still having trouble keeping food down. Exercise is progressing, and F took a trip outside the hospital in a wheelchair this week. Today (Sunday) planning a trip out in the car too.

Hello guys -
Many, many thanks once again for all your emails and pictures - superb red tractor James, I'm not even going to try to compete. Brendan - I'm definitely considering installing myself permanently in the boathouse, probably wrapped in a tartan blanket and wearing some sort of smoking cap. Jim - I'm holding off my entrance into a state of infinite happiness until I can get my trousers on by myself - hope this is alright? And Clare - how much we'd both like to be in a hail-stoned Norway - it sounds as if you're having a great time.

News here - well, I'm now wearing CLOTHES - fetching combination of tracksuit bottoms and one of Alun's mum's polka dot tops. Very chic/schik. Can also get round a bit more. Have been measured for a pressure suit (kind of lycra corset - perhaps I'll end up looking like that sexy alien from Star Trek? There's always hope . . .[and F doesn't like sci-fi? ed.]) which I'll have to wear for A WHOLE YEAR. Am getting my even more attractive plastic mask next week - a pretty appalling prospect, but everyone here raves about the results, so I'll have to grin and bear it. Am going to have to invest in a range of interesting hats. Or perhaps convert to Islam? One of those black, billowing burqa would come in handy. Anyway - once again I must sign off, poor old Alun is going to type all this up. He's drawing a picture of me at the moment, to give you an idea of my present eccentric coiffure!

Lots of love to you all, as ever, xxx F
Sunday 21 - Tuesday 23 September

F walking without zimmer, and only shoulder support! No oxygen mask required now, and nasal feed will be stopped shortly. There's a kind of catch 22 going on whereby the nasal feed seems to make F throw up, which means she needs a nasal feed to make up the calorific intake required!

On a lighter note, the trusty Cavalier made its last journey yesterday, to the scrap yard. Just after delivering me safely to Selly Oak it started smoking (appropriately enough in the designated smoking area outside the hospital). It's done a fantastic extra 7,000 miles this summer, and I don't even have a photo of it :-(
As a result Faith now has some important and useful additions to her room, a car CD player, foot pump, battery charger, extra blankets and de-icer. This has cheered her up greatly.
Saturday 20 September

Very little use of oxygen mask these days now, so the only remaining tube is the nasal feeding tube. They've got F sitting up in a chair again and walking round the room with support and a zimmer frame. The only problem slowing down this progress now is a possible 'calcified' knee, but this will go with more exercise, albeit painfully. So everything is looking so good right now. We're even able to begin thinking of where on earth we could live, what with the house being blown up an' all!

Brief aside: Hill Top Hospital (3.20 ITV Mon - Fri) is absolutely excellent, and despite its lack of a Burns Unit, is a pretty accurate depiction of life at Selly Oak!

Yes indeed, yesterday F honestly did write her own diary entry, hand-written and typed up by me. But you'll never really know will you?
Friday 19 September

Hello everyone - I feel a bit overwhelmed after having read Alun's compendious day to day account of parts of my life that I'd almost forgotten (or wasn't awake enough to appreciate). Did I really ask for Fame Academy to be turned off? Shame on me! But yes - I am prepared to admit my ongoing addiction to Sex and the City and almost any crappy interior design or gardening programme going. While I couldn't eat this also extended to cookery shows (I'd start salivating at the mere sight of Ainsley Herriot - yes, it really was that bad . . . )

Needless to say, I've been evermore overwhelmed by the immense kindness everyone has shown me during this whole trauma - I really don't know how to thank you all enough for your thoughts, prayers, words, and (of course) emails. It's odd, but this has made what is in some ways a terrible experience into one with many, many positives. I'm still a bit shy about visitors - but they're planning to let me out on day release (à la prison) as soon as I can do more than circle the room with the aid of a physiotherapist and a zimmer frame. So who knows? That long-awaited trip to Alton Towers perhaps?

Right, must stop now as dinner has arrived, and Alun is distracting me with his raucous laughter at the latest offering from the Simpsons.
Love to you all,
Faith
Thursday 18 September (12 weeks)

Yep, trachy tube out! Mask still required most of the time to up the supply of oxygen, especially as F starts to exercise now and will get out of breath quickly.
Saturday 13 - Wednesday 17 September

F is due to have her trachy tube out today (Wednesday) which should be a great relief.

On Monday F went to theatre to have her face measured up for a mask. She will possibly have to wear one for a few months. This is only to keep pressure on the skin, because the biggest thing with burns scars is that grafts can tend to contract unless pressure is kept on them for quite a while after surgery. This is the reason for both the mask and the 'pressure' garments that F will wear for up to two years from now. We haven't seen the mask, but it's apparently like the one Pam Warren wore after the Paddington disaster - transparent and tight fitting and actually reasonably discreet.

Wednesday 10 - Friday 12 September

Lots of physio, general arm waving and pushing a balloon around the bed (?). Had a go with some kid's coloured marker pens, and started getting creative. As a result Faith now wants to decorate the room with drawings by you all - so another method for you to send your wishes, messages.

Try the online upload thingy if you don't mind us all seeing your work - it could be photos or anything though. Otherwise send original drawings/photos to the Burns Unit. I'll scan in Faith's pics when I get a chance.
Tuesday 9 September

Spoken to doctors now, and there is some news on the estimated schedule. Don't take this as gospel, but if all goes well we can expect F to be in Selly Oak for at least a further 6 weeks, and more likely 2 months. The only other Burns Unit she could move to that might be nearer Oxford would be Stoke Mandeville, and that's not appreciably nearer really. Quite apart from that it would mean a change of team which would be upsetting for Faith. So - keep those news updates coming for a while yet! Today F enjoyed marmite and cucumber sandwiches :-)
Monday 8 September
Thanks J for suggestion of universal remote control! The TV in the unit didn't have a remote, and previously I had to leave on the most promising channel for the evening, (usually Channel Five!) when I left. F can now change channels at will. She still has a couple of chest infections, so progress is slow.
Saturday 6 - Sunday 7 September

F safely out of surgery - and more bandaged up than ever now which unfortunately restricts movement even more. One curious (beneficial?) side effect of the drugs is a dislike for anything sweet. Lime cordial and grapefruit are going down really well though.

Friday 5 September

Surgery over - F out of it all day really now. Expect to get word from the docs on Saturday, but she's safe and sound back in the Burns Unit.
Latest listening: Milt Jackson Sunflower; Ry Cooder with V.M.Bhatt A Meeting by the River
Wednesday 3 - Thursday 4 September

Cough improved, and going into surgery on the Friday now. Faith managing bits of tuna sandwich - so not just pureed food. No indication yet of when the trachy tube might come out. F very impressed by look of new Selfridges building, and may never leave Birmingham as a result.
Tuesday 2 September

No theatre after all today - chest a bit too 'tacky' as they call it. Faith's still not on to the talking books folks - it's still too hard to control the player at the moment. Had a speaking tube in on Monday which worked this time - it's been in before but Faith didn't have the technique or strength to work it. Basically it's a valve in the trachy tube that allows some air to pass over the vocal chords. Quite incredible to hear her voice again after so long. It's not in for long though, as it takes more effort to control. That and her general weakness are the reasons for her continuing to restrict visitors.

Thursday 28 - Monday 1 September

All continues to be well. Possibility of further grafting on Tuesday, and the trachy tube is now on its smallest size - next step is to remove it, but no date for that yet.

Wednesday 27 August

Faith says she loves you all! She can also now touch her nose and is eating food - the hospital thankfully has a menu that includes dal!
Monday - Tuesday 25 - 26 August

A dream from Intensive Care:
Faith has asked me to write up a dream/hallucination/false memory she had whilst in Intensive Care. When she awoke she was convinced this was true, and had quite a few problems distinguishing real memories from imagined ones for a while.
In a gallery when a famous female YBA (young british artist) turns up. She starts drawing Faith, but the drawing is really crap. She was going to show it at a private view at the gallery that evening, so Faith stays for that. At the PV everyone else thought the drawing was pretty bad too. There were two parts to the gallery, F in the smaller, less busy part. Realised she was sitting in an airplane seat,looked up and saw that that small bit of the gallery was like part of a plane which was flying to Paris after the PV. F panicked, as she didn't have a passport or any money - so asked if they were going to stop anywhere. The plane did stop, in a field with a chateau. The captain argued with the crew about something, so the plane just sat there all day in the field. Meanwhile some local gypsy children came and stole all the pre-packed meals. Others on the plane noted that this was a great example of the French working class spirit.
F at this point was sick and needed a blood transfusion, but the woman who was supposed to be helping her was in fact trying to kill her. She shut F up in a small cubicle and walked around it with a scaffolding pole, which was very scary, and told everyone F was a pathological liar who had killed her sister. Bit forgotten here, but eventually F escaped and got to Paris. The press were waiting and the only person F could think of who could help was Julian [F been on a recent trip to Paris with Julian, ed]. She found some friends of his working in an eco-shop - they were going to print the story and get themselves a news scoop. By the way they also ran an Indian puppet theatre. F agreed to meet Julian so he could drive her back to the UK, but she missed the appointment because she couldn't move. He was extremely cross, but Faith couldn't explain the reason. Three other friends of Julian's had a boat and agreed to take F back to the UK. They had a beautiful trip and at the end were picked up by a navy vessel which had nurses on board. The End!
Wednesday 20 August to Sunday 24 August

Faith continuing physio, moving hands and arms about. Slight chest infection is frustrating her, as it involves a lot of very tiring coughing, but the doctors are happy with progress. She still has the trachy and the oxygen mask, but has started eating a little bit of pureed food, horrible though it tastes. Even food she was looking forward to tastes bad, partly as a result of the drugs.

For future, I think I can say that if there's no news on the site, don't worry! It's likely just to be because everything is OK and I haven't made it to the office computer in time. I should explain my situation a bit for those of you who've been trying to contact me, or have contacted me and it's been brief. I'm working out my last week of a part-time job in the mornings, where I have no mobile reception., then I'm in the car up to Brum, and can't talk, not just because my hands free set keeps breaking, but because it's soon to be illegal, then I'm at the hospital where mobiles are not allowed, then back in the car, then I'm living with my folks, where I have no mobile reception (O2 btw). Thank goodness for modern technology eh? From September, I'll be back to working for myself in Oxford, and should have phone reception. So - keep the calls / news emails coming, but please don't expect a reply for a good while yet and don't take it personally.
Tuesday 19 August

Physios preparing a gruelling exercise regime to get those muscles back again.

Monday 18 August

Faith now safely up on the Burns Unit. Most tubes are out - but Faith's still not taking food and will be on the trachy for a bit longer with an oxygen mask.

Sunday 17 August

Quiet day, all going well. Sorry none of you are getting replies to your emails, but rest assured they're all being read by Faith now and much appreciated. Until she can talk and receive visitors these are a brilliant way of finding out about the outside world and all her friends. Please do keep them coming.
To give you an idea, it's a huge effort at the moment for Faith just to raise her hand off the bed. It can be hard to appreciate just how weak she is.
Saturday 16 August

Sorry, false alert - they planned to move Faith, but she's not gone yet. Will be in Intensive Care for the weekend now.

Friday 15 August

Good news - Faith moved out of Intensive Care back to Burns in the afternoon. Excuse the briefer and more erratic updates - things are a bit busy!
Wednesday 13 and Thursday 14 August (7 weeks)
Continuing the weaning off the ventilator. Most of Thursday spent actually on a mask over the tracheotomy.
Tuesday 12 August

Steady progress. Faith still often very tired, but really enjoying reading the emails that you're all so kindly sending in. Hold off on tapes etc for now - we've got a big backlog, as Faith is still not listening to much.
Monday 11 August

Good quiet day.
Sunday 10 August

A long day with the doctors, changing her dressings again. The dressings have now actually been removed from her chest and back. A good day, increasing the time spent on what they call CPAP, which is still support from the ventilator, but minimal. Keen people can look it up!

Saturday 9 August

Another good day of sitting up in this chair contraption, watching Home and Away, and gradual weaning off the ventilator (reducing the number of hours Faith has mechanical support for her lungs).
Friday 8 August

Faith sat up again this morning, and leg dressings were removed temporarily. Drank some blackcurrent juice, and will be allowed ice cream tomorrow! Three hours or more of breathing for herself, so every day gets a little bit easier. Letters read, some music played, some Simpsons watched, and other programmes earmarked for later viewing - I'm hesitant of displaying our tastes publically like this after various comments I've received (heh), but Sex and the City is lined up, and ER - but not Holby City (phew). I'm glad this isn't a blogger where people can leave comments, or it would end up as a cultural boxing ring.
Thursday 7 August (6 weeks)

Faith was put in a kind of chair this morning - her first time in the full six weeks! They'll try to do this every day now, and apparently the physio is getting pretty rough - they had her knees up to her nose, and I've never been able to do that, but then I dropped yoga after three weeks! One hour breathing for herself is followed by an hour with the ventilator - all it's doing now though is kick starting her inhalation, as her muscles still get very tired. Still not really interested in music or talking books quite yet, but we watched the Simpsons and chose programmes she'd like to watch over the week. The great thing about this tracheotomoty is that once she's off the ventilation, her oxygen mask will go over the throat rather the mouth, making speaking much easier.
Wednesday 6 August

Back to a couple of hours breathing for herself, then a couple of hours support from the ventilator. I do ask every day if she'd like visitors, but so far the answer is no until she can talk and breath on her own. She's obviously exhausted even after I've been in there five minutes - trying to respond to people is hard work when you're still struggling to learn to breath again. Mostly her parents and I go in and hold up emails and cards for Faith to read until she's too tired, or just chat amongst ourselves quietly.
Tuesday 5 August

Faith wide awake today, still breathing with help from the ventilator. Can't talk but can mouth some words, though it's pretty hard going. Now she's wide awake she's experiencing a bit of frustration.
Monday 4 August

Faith was put back on the ventilator last night, and went to theatre this afternoon for the tracheotomy (theatre necessary because she had a vein near her larynx which was tricky). She was sedated for theatre, but should be awake again tomorrow, and ventilated through the hole in her windpipe now. This actually can be more comfortable as her mouth will be free of tubes and the mask. She won't be able to talk, though she might mouth words, nor eat yet. Really the picture is that she wasn't quite strong enough to breathe for herself and expel the secretions from her infections. Things should improve again now, but there will be a repeat of the 'weaning' process to get her breathing for herself fully again.
Sunday 3 August

Faith had a temperature today, and wasn't quite strong enough to cough up secretions caused by a new infection. As a result the doctors are contemplating putting her back on the ventilator tonight and carrying out a tracheotomy / tracheostomy (*) to get rid of the secretions. She will be sedated again (unconscious) if they do intubate her again.
Saturday 2 August

Theatre was fine - haven't seen the Burns doctors at all, but all seems well. Today, big event, the ventilator was removed! Faith extremely happy to be without that tube down her. As a result of the long period of sedation and immobility, she's got a lot of secretion on her chest which she needs to cough up, and is far too hoarse to speak. She's still on an oxygen mask, but by the end of the day she was at least herself enough to agree that Fame Academy should be switched OFF (not sure why it was on in the first place)! Had first drink of water too, and mouthed that she wanted to walk already - no chance of that yet I'm afraid, after 28 days in ICU - but they will encourage her to exercise as much as possible. The stress now is on making sure Faith's breathing stays regular.
Friday 1 August

Going to theatre today after all, for further grafting, which means the Burns doctors are happy with the progress so far. However, they'll keep the ventilator in today as a result. Some hallucinatory episodes (see Wednesday), but Faith was talked through these and was at least aware of the fact that they were not reality.
Thursday 31 July (5 weeks)

Still slowly progressing, the doctors are still saying tomorrow for the ventilator removal. They are cautious because they don't want to have to put it back in later - insertion and removal are major means of introducing further infections. Read out some letters, and Faith enjoyed reading others herself when I held them up, which must be a relief - at last having some control over your own world. We left Alan Bennett - Chip in the Sugar - playing on a portable tape deck at Faith's request (ran through a couple of the titles). Keep those great gossipy / newsy letters coming!
Wednesday 30 July

Faith still aware with moments of drowsiness and confusion (post ICU psychosis possibly - a real term *). Didn't want music on or talking books unfortunately - I think all effort is still focused on breathing and recovery, and our time in ICU is perhaps more stressful for her as she tries to talk. With luck she will after all come off the ventilator tomorrow which may help with this. Gossipy news reading has been well received thanks.
Tuesday 29 July

Slightly over-optimistic report yesterday perhaps, Faith still on the ventilator and might be for a few days yet. Everything else is stable and good. Ironically she's not getting much sleep at the moment, so is still looking confused and wide-eyed. Looking into the possibility of having a proper portable CD player in the Unit, which would get rid of the need for headphones (which get in the way and fall off etc). However, I'm very concerned about Faith's aesthetic sense being offended by my more chaotic approach to design. Does anyone have any recommendations for a designer-but-not-too-posh-looking-because-it-might-get-stolen-or-damaged portable CD/tape/radio player with remote control and headphone jack?!
Monday 28 July

Faith breathing for herself although the ventilation tube remains in place providing support. Alert and responsive and had some sleep last night apparently - you wouldn't think she needed it! No theatre was due today after all, and there won't be any for a bit (see below). Cross your fingers - if all remains well they are planning to take Faith off ventilation tomorrow. This rapid change in the last couple of days is still hard to appreciate.
Musical choice: compilation tape Faith herself made in 1995-ish. Gossipy news is sought.
Don't be surprised if this diary format changes soon, depending on Faith's wishes - she may be able to dictate entries herself or may prefer it to go offline, we'll have to see.
Medical bit (avoid if squeemish): apparently a thin layer of skin has been taken from her abdomen and hips and used for grafting onto her left side. This re-grows in about 10 days, and then they can take more skin from the same area. These so-called "donor" sites can be more painful than the original burns.
Sunday 27 July

Faith spending longer periods off ventilation, and still alert.

Saturday 26 July

Blimey!
Faith came off sedation at 12 noon, and when I saw her at 3pm she was blinking and occassionally responding to questions with nods and wiggling her feet. She's on ventilation two hours out of every three, so learning again to breathe for herself in the other hour (with the tube still in place). It's bound to be a very weird period emerging from three weeks of unconsciousness, but she definitely nodded her head at the idea of playing her Chill-Out by the KLF when I left!
All this has happened sooner than expected, and is a really good sign that her condition has improved dramatically. Again, there are the usual warnings that she's still extremely susceptible to infection at this stage, and still has a way to go before she's fully breathing for herself and able to talk. However - I think everyone should raise a glass to Faith now - I know I have (quite a few times).
She's due into theatre again on Monday to have all her dressings changed.

Friday 25 July

Theatre day. Faith was well enough to go into theatre in the afternoon. The surgeons grafted skin from her leg onto her forehead, and from her left arm onto her right arm. Haven't managed to speak to any doctors but I imagine this is the stage of treatment where the epidermal layer of the skin is grafted (this site on Integra is pretty good for an explanation) over the Integra.
Thursday 24 July (Four weeks)

Things still improving: they are allowing Faith to breathe a little for herself, and are readying her for theatre tomorrow (to carry out further grafting onto the integra on the chest - a good sign). I always err on the cautious side, but the phrase "turned the corner" has been used ! Just another reminder though, Faith is now, and has been for the last three weeks, unconscious.
No update tomorrow as I'm overnighting.

Wednesday 23 July

Good stable day - if things carry on like this, the surgeons want to take Faith into theatre on Friday. This is really just to check up on her and is a good thing.

Tuesday 22 July

p.m. CT scan didn't show any progression of lung damage - so that's good. Although it must be stressed that Faith is still in an extremely critical condition, the nurse on today last saw her two weeks ago and is confident that she's doing better than she was then, so progress has been made.
a.m. Please note, I am NOT censoring music ! Faith undergoing a CT (Computed Tomography Imaging, also CAT) Scan today.
Monday 21 July

Another stable day, figures for O2 and CO2 are lower which is good. Saw one of the plastic surgeons today who was pleased with the progress of Faith's wounds - and apparently her hands are very mobile (with physio). Just a quick note to remind people that Faith is still under heavy sedation and has been artificially paralysed to encourage her body to concentrate on the lung repair. She is still a fairly long way off being able to breathe for herself or being conscious. Suzanne Vega this evening. You'll be glad to hear the Alan Bennett has been approved even though some stories are pretty sad!
Sunday 20 July

No change today, Faith pretty much in same condition. While her CO2 is still at 18% they are not overly concerned. Almost all her own outer layer of skin has grown back now on her face. Left Bach on the tape. Am pre-listening to various Bennett tapes to make sure there's nothing too depressing to listen to, hope this censorship is ok!

Saturday 19 July

Spoke to today's doctor (there are quite a few on rotation between two hospitals in Birmingham, so we've hardly spoken to the same one twice). The picture is getting a little bit clearer to us (and probably to them). It seems the intial explosion caused more damage than was at first thought. This led to inflammation in the lungs, and consequent lung damage, pneumothorax (*). This means a portion of her left lung has hardened and is not functioning. This is not too damaging by itself in a healthy person, but obviously Faith is in a severely critical state, so it's causing the complications. The infections picked up two weeks ago exacerbated the problem, but are not now the principal cause of concern. Her O2 and CO2 levels continue to fluctuate daily, but all without affecting her badly at the moment - her other organs are functioning fine. However it now seems the oscillator may have made little difference after all (sorry to fans of the oscillator, but to be honest it didn't look very hi-tech!). So the situation remains very serious and we and the doctors can only hope the lung repairs itself soon. Thomas Tallis left playing on the Walkman.
I've extracted some notes on respiration from a good site here.
Friday 18 July

Stable today, same condition as yesterday. Unfortunately a planned trip was cancelled due to small problem with car breaking down (luckily still in Oxford). Something to do with Distributor Caps - all VERY MYSTERIOUS, but the Binckes Cavalier will be back on the road tomorrow.
Thursday 17 July (Three weeks)

Up and down day, started with a bad patch, CO2 back up and oxygen feed up, but by 7pm Faith was taken off the oscillator and was back on the normal ventilator. I was wrong about the oscillator, it's actually a much harsher method to encourage breathing, so it's a good thing to be back on the ventilator. Everyone on list below just about tracked down.
Wednesday 16 July

Oscillator doing a bit of a better job this morning (and stable through the day) - CO2 levels right down at last (6%). But keep praying and hang on in there for a further couple of weeks folks - it's expected to be a long haul. On the burns front, Faith's face is really clearing up now, almost three weeks in. More people are still sending new messages in which is fantastic. I'm only sorry some of you heard the news so late in the day.
Tuesday 15 July

No change today - CO2 still at 20% level.
Monday 14 July

No real change today again, except that Faith's now on an oscillator, a gentler kind of respirator to help clear CO2 from her lungs. We're calling for music tapes at the moment, as the nurses are happy to play her Walkman to her while she is sedated. Not hundreds, but a fine selection would be good. I'll put up a list of what she's liked recently.

I think there are a couple of people Faith knows that may not know the news yet. If any of you out there know where they are, please let them know: Clare Bradshaw, Simon James, Grant Somebody, David Somebody (in Cheltenham?), Lorenzo. Thanks Liz for seeking out some of these and hello Anne!
Saturday 12 and Sunday 13 July
Faith still in the same condition as Friday, no change. The current best prognosis is that Faith will be under sedation for a further three weeks at least if she can repair the damage to her lungs caused by inflammation. But really things have to taken day by day at the moment. As a result I'm still travelling up and down from Birmingham, so available in Oxford at times.
Friday 11 July
p.m. Faith is unfortunately in a very critical condition at the moment. The anaesthetist explained that Faith is still fighting the infection/s in her chest but is now on 90% oxygen and doing very little for herself. I expect to be staying in Birmingham from tomorrow for a bit so this site may be only erratically updated. Faith's got an enormous quantity of wonderful cards which will take me some time to read out to her.

Thursday 10 July (Two weeks)
Faith had a bronchoscopy (*) this lunchtime to check her lungs. She's been having a bit more trouble breathing, and is on 65% oxygen on the ventilator. The results were clear, ie there's no evidence of any build up in her lungs. However the breathing problems mean she may well not go to surgery on Friday, but in any case I've now been told she'll probably be under sedation and on a ventilator for another week to deal with this infection.
Wednesday 9 July
Still stable. The doctors are planning surgery for Friday (normal theatre days are Tuesdays and Fridays for burns). This is the surgery that was started last week, and is basically to graft skin onto the backs of Faith's legs. All other major grafting has been done (during the major Saturday operation).
Tuesday 8 July
The doctors report no change today, all is stable. Thanks to all of you again for your continued support. When she wakes up she'll be swamped with messages of love.
Monday 7 July
Faith is still under sedation and on a respirator, though mostly to help her own breathing which is pretty strong. The doctor thought she would be on sedation all this week in Intensive Care while the antibiotics take effect. They should begin to take action today. I guess this means no visitors for at least another week folks, and no talking books required yet.

Weekend spent on respirator and sedated while dealing with the chest infection.
Friday 4 July

Faith drinks coffee (still with one sugar I'm afraid), a little bit of soup, and writes first words - "Put the Kettle On" - for some reason!
Wednesday 2 July
Faith moves up to Burns Unit, looked after mostly by Mark and Carl, army medics who are brill. She has some raspberry smoothie.
Tuesday 1 July
Second operation to carry out grafting on legs. Operation stopped after small reaction to the anaesthetic. All well otherwise, and operation postponed until next week. Surgeons still very happy.
Monday 30 June
Off the respirator, breathing with mask and able to talk more, but very very quietly.
Sunday 29 June
Pretty hard day recovering and on respirator.
Saturday 28 June
7 - 8 hour surgery, where most of grafting is carried out.
Friday 27 June
Long day in Intensive Care. Parents come up to visit.
Thursday 26 June
Faith in gas explosion at 39 Greenridges, Oxford. She's taken to the John Radcliffe Hospital by 11.30am, and moved on to Birmingham at 9pm. She's taken to theatre about 1am for examination and cleaning, and is out in the Intensive Care Unit by 4am Friday.
